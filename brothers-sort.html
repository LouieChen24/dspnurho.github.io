BROTHER SPOTLIGHT ON INTERNSHIP HACKS

I think the biggest tip that I can give about finding an internship is to be extremely persistent. Don't just limit yourself to a few applications; apply to everything you think you might have the slightest bit of interest in and definitely network and utilize your communications. Delta Sigma Pi taught me how to present myself professionally, and that carried through from the moment I interviewed, to my daily presence at work and my presentation skills in front of VPs and Directors.

Tiffany Lin, Summer Finance Intern at Barclays

Treat an interview as if it were a formal conversation with one of your friends and it will be much easier to explain to the interviewer your experiences and qualifications. Try to sound as natural as possible, and don't be afraid to show a little bit of passion throughout the interview! To be honest, I was completely clueless concerning anything internship related prior to joining Delta Sigma Pi. The fraternity and its brothers not only taught me how to write a resume and cover letter, but also gave me hands-on experience in how to converse with business professionals in both an interview and a non-interview setting.

Cory Chen, Summer Accounting Intern at AirBnB

Make sure that there are no errors in your resume, cover letter, presentations, and emails. I did not believe having attention-to-detail was this crucial, but after networking and interviewing with HP managers, every one of them emphasized the importance of being error-free. I can say that Delta Sigma Pi has definitely helped me in this aspect, and because of this skill, I've had the opportunity to present in front of eight executives and directors.

Leslie Chan, Summer Supply Chain Analyst Intern at Hewlett Packard Enterprise
