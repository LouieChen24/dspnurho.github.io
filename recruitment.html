WHY JOIN DELTA SIGMA PI?

One of the advantages of joining Delta Sigma Pi is having the chance to participate in the pledging program. This program offers individuals the unique opportunity to operate in an environment that promotes rapid growth and encourages exploration into a variety of skill sets. In addition to curating your interviewing skills, event-planning experience, and public speaking skills, you have the chance to network across a wide range of individuals, ranging from fellow students to alumni to business owners. Through pledging, you will learn and grow alongside your pledge brothers, and have access to a support system that will push you to constantly learn and take on new things. The relationships you create and the professional skills you gain will carry forward throughout your collegiate experience and beyond.

WHAT YOU CAN EXPECT FROM US

Case Competitions

Each year, we partner with companies like Deloitte, Equilar, Target, VSP, and AT&T to bring our trademark Case Competitions to UC Davis. All teams have a chance to win internships and cash prizes with these companies.

Campus Engagement

Deltasigs are campus leaders and self-starters. Our brothers have founded rising student organizations such as the Davis Consulting Group, a student-run consulting organization, and IGNITE, a civic and political organization for women.

Company Relations

We have toured various companies including Pinterest, Blackrock, LinkedIn, and Tesla and have held private company information sesions with businesses like Accenture, Deloitte, Target, AT&T, and Equilar.

Alumni Mentorship

We take pride in our 21 years of Delta Sigma Pi alumni who return to the UC Davis campus each year to share their stories, give career advice, mentor our current brothers, and stay involved with our Chapter.

FAQ

How can joining business fraternity enhance my college experience?

You should join a business fraternity in order to gain professional development outside the classroom setting. While it is possible to obtain various internships and professional knowledge without the aid of a professional business fraternity, becoming a brother has many benefits, including opening the door to our vast alumni network in various industries, access to professional, company recruitment, and community service events to further enrich the college experience. Brotherhood is an experience that cannot be mimicked, and is one of the most important aspects of our fraternity. Once you join Delta Sigma Pi, you are a brother for life.

What is the pledging program?

The pledge program is designed to build brotherhood and professionalism and prepare each pledge to become a brother in the fraternity. Pledges receive an opportunity to gain exposure to various aspects of the business world through workshops, presentations, interview practice, and more. The time commitment is equivalent to a part-time internship and lasts roughly around eight weeks.

I have applied the previous term, but did not receive a bid. Should I reapply?

Absolutely! Several brothers have applied more than once before receiving bids. We look at repeated applications as a sign of dedication, persistence, and commitment. Be sure to illustrate how you have developed since last applying and what you have done to get closer to accomplishing your goals!

How many bids does Delta Sigma Pi give out each Recruitment Drive?

There is no fixed number of candidates we take each term. The size of the pledge class depends on the quality of the applicant pool. Pledge classes have ranged from 6 to 16 pledges in past terms.

What criteria is considered when evaluating applicants?

We evaluate candidates based on their application, culture fit, and performance during the professional interview. We look for candidates who are looking to improve themselves both professionally and personally.

Do I have to be a Managerial Economics or other business-related major to join?

We are a business-focused Fraternity for business or business-intended students. However, our brothers have incorporated various majors such as Communications, International Relations, Sociology - Organizational Studies, Design, and more into their pursuit of business. Candidates are also evaluated on a holistic basis, and there are occasional exceptions.
